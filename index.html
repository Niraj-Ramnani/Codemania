History

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, commemorates the victory of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. This ancient festival has its roots in Hindu mythology and is celebrated with great fervor across India, highlighting its cultural and historical importance..

Significance

Dussehra signifies the victory of righteousness and the importance of moral values in our lives. It serves as a reminder to uphold truth and justice, encouraging individuals to reflect on their own actions and strive for virtue in their daily lives.
